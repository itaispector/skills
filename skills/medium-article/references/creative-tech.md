# Music Production / Creative Tech — Domain Reference

## Audience assumptions

This is a crossover audience: developers who make music, or musicians who code. They might use a DAW daily but also open a terminal. They appreciate when someone bridges the gap between creative intuition and technical precision. Don't write like a music magazine and don't write like a programming tutorial — write like someone who lives in both worlds.

## Current discourse landscape

- The DAW landscape is mature but Logic Pro, Ableton, and FL Studio each have strong communities with different cultures
- Plugin ecosystem is massive — readers are skeptical of "best plugin" claims
- AI in music production is a hot topic (stem separation, mastering, generation) with strong opinions on both sides
- The loudness wars are over but mastering philosophy still sparks debate
- Analog vs digital is largely settled (digital won for most use cases) but analog emulation quality matters
- The bedroom producer → professional pipeline is more accessible than ever

## Strong angles for this domain

- Technical deep dives that musicians don't usually get (DSP concepts explained through code, how compressors actually work under the hood)
- Workflow optimization — the intersection of engineering thinking and creative process
- Honest plugin/tool reviews from someone who understands both the audio and the software
- The developer's perspective on music production (automation, version control for projects, reproducibility)
- Building custom tools for music (MIDI controllers, audio plugins, Max/MSP, Web Audio API)
- Genre-specific production techniques with actual signal chain breakdowns

## Writing cautions

- **Don't be a gear snob.** Good music comes from cheap gear constantly. Focus on technique and taste.
- **Be specific about signal chains.** "I used compression" is useless. "FabFilter Pro-C 2 in Opto mode, 3:1 ratio, slow attack to let the transient through" is useful.
- **Acknowledge subjectivity in taste.** Technical facts are facts, but "this sounds better" is an opinion — own it as one.
- **Don't gatekeep.** Whether someone uses Splice loops or records live instruments, the craft is in the arrangement and mixing.

## Vocabulary notes

- Use genre-specific terms correctly: "melodic techno" and "melodic house" are distinct genres
- "Mix" (noun/verb) vs "master" (noun/verb) — don't conflate them
- "DAW" is universally understood, no need to expand
- "Signal chain" / "signal flow" — use these, they're precise
- "Sound design" means creating sounds from scratch, not choosing presets
- "Gain staging" not "volume management"

## Title examples that work

- "How I Use Engineering Thinking to Mix Melodic House"
- "The FabFilter Pro-Q 4 Dynamic EQ Changed How I Approach Low-End"
- "Building a Custom MIDI Controller with Arduino — A Producer's Perspective"
- "Why Your Master Sounds Flat (And It's Probably Not the Limiter)"
